# Product Discovery Report Template

Use this template to guide each agent's output and assemble the final report.

## Report Header

```markdown
# {PRODUCT_NAME}: Full Agency Discovery Exercise

> **Exercise type:** Multi-agent product discovery
> **Date:** {DATE}
> **Agents deployed:** 8 (in parallel)
> **Purpose:** Comprehensive product blueprint from idea to execution plan

---
```

## Section Deliverables

### Section 1: Executive Summary (Executive Summary Generator)
- Concise 1-page executive summary of all findings
- Key metrics snapshot (market size, budget range)
- GO / NO-GO / CONDITIONAL verdict with top 3 reasons
- Top 3 risks and top 3 opportunities
- Critical next steps

### Section 2: The Opportunity (written by orchestrator)
- How the idea originated / what problem it solves
- The core concept in 2-3 sentences
- Why now — what market conditions make this timely
- Target users

### Section 3: Market Validation (Trend Researcher)
- Market size tables (TAM/SAM/SOM with sources)
- Competitive landscape table (tool, strength, gap)
- Market gaps and unmet needs
- Verdict: GO / NO-GO / CONDITIONAL with reasoning

### Section 4: Product Strategy & Requirements (Product Manager Agent)
- Problem statement and value proposition
- User stories for core flows (prioritized MoSCoW)
- MVP scope definition (in/out table)
- Success metrics and KPIs
- Product roadmap with relative milestones (Phase 1, 2, 3 — no fixed dates)

### Section 5: Brand Strategy (Brand Guardian)
- Positioning statement
- Name candidates and rationale
- Visual identity direction (colors, typography, style)
- Brand voice and tone guidelines
- Competitive differentiation narrative

### Section 6: Go-to-Market & Growth (Growth Hacker)
- GTM strategy and launch phases
- Pricing model and tiers
- Key acquisition channels ranked by expected ROI
- Viral/referral mechanics
- KPIs and growth targets by phase

### Section 7: Legal & Compliance (Legal Compliance Checker)
- Regulatory landscape (GDPR, CCPA, industry-specific)
- Data protection and privacy requirements
- IP considerations (patents, trademarks, open-source licensing)
- Terms of service and user agreement needs
- Compliance risks ranked by severity

### Section 8: Project Execution Plan (Project Shepherd)
- Phase breakdown with relative milestones
- Sprint-level backlog (first 2-3 sprints detailed)
- Team composition and roles needed
- Risk register with mitigation strategies
- Dependencies

### Section 9: Domain-Specific (varies by product)
- Architecture and design specific to the product's domain
- Domain-specific technical considerations
- Integration points with the core system
- Domain best practices and patterns

### Section 10: Cross-Agent Synthesis (written by orchestrator)
- **Agreements:** Where 3+ agents converge on the same conclusion
- **Tensions:** Where agents disagree or recommend conflicting approaches
- **Top Risks:** Risks identified by multiple agents, ranked by severity
- **Final Recommendation:** GO / NO-GO / CONDITIONAL with conditions
- **Phased Roadmap:** Integrated roadmap combining all agent recommendations (relative milestones, no fixed dates)
